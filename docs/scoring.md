# Scoring Philosophy

## Core Principles

The scoring model is deterministic, transparent, and role-centric.

- Deterministic means the same inputs always produce the same outputs.
- Transparent means each score maps back to readable profile or team context inputs.
- Role-centric means the model evaluates usage quality and environment instead of forecasting fantasy production.

## Score Definitions

### Role Value

Role value measures how important the player's receiving job appears to be. The score emphasizes target share, route participation, first-read involvement, red-zone usage, air-yard command, and versatility. Archetypes like `WR1` and `TE1` receive modest deterministic bonuses because those roles typically imply stronger offensive priority.

### Opportunity Quality

Opportunity quality measures the health of the team passing ecosystem. It rewards pass-friendly environments, strong red-zone passing behavior, stable quarterback situations, continuity in play-calling, and lower target competition.

### Role Stability

Role stability measures how likely the role is to remain secure week to week. It rewards high route participation, stable team infrastructure, clearer receiver-room hierarchy, lower competition, and lower injury risk.

### Vacated Opportunity

Vacated opportunity estimates how much unclaimed receiving work could realistically flow toward the role. It blends the player's access to available targets, team-level vacated share, and the player's versatility to absorb multiple alignments or assignments.

## Interpreting Score Bands

- `80-100`: strong signal, favorable role or environment.
- `65-79`: clearly positive signal with some constraints.
- `50-64`: mixed or situational profile.
- `0-49`: weak signal, uncertain role, or poor team context.
